At-Risk Communities in Flint, Michigan

Mastery Assignment 3

Posted by Beth Lawson on December 4, 2025

In 2014, the city of Flint, Michigan began sourcing its city water supply from the Flint River, despite a decades-long history of industrial and raw sewage dumping.

In less than two years, this move proved to be one of the worst human-made public health crises in recent American memory.

SHAME. SHAME. SHAME!

What story does this map tell?

This map displays the communities most at-risk for lead exposure in the wake of the move to shift water sourcing. With this information, local and state governments, along with NGOs can determine which areas to target for public health interventions. As children in particular are vulnerable to high levels of lead exposure, maps like this can also identify areas in need of more intensive early intervention tactics to lessen the impacts of such exposure.
